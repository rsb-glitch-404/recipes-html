Tiramisu!

About: Tiramisu (Italian: tiramis√π) is an Italian dessert. The name comes from the Italian tirami su, translating to 'pick me up' or 'cheer me up' It is made of ladyfinger pastries (savoiardi) dipped in coffee, layered with a whipped mixture of eggs, sugar, and mascarpone and flavoured with cocoa. The recipe has been adapted into many varieties of cakes and other desserts. These ingredients are just a standard outline, not a hard and fast specification Its origin is disputed between the Italian regions of Veneto and Friuli-Venezia Giulia, nonetheless it's a dessert enjoyed by all. Oftentimes, alcohol will be added to the cake for adults to enjoy further The following method and ingredients lists will be from the version I have made.

Ingredients:
  1. The Tiramisu Cream
    • 250 grams of Mascarpone Cheese
    • 25 grams of Sweet Condensed Milk
    • 1 teaspoon of Vanilla Extract
    • 200 grams of Heavy Cream
    • 20 grams of Sugar
  2. Coffee Syrup
    • 120 grams of Black Coffee
    • 15g of Sugar
    • Kahlua/Baileys/Another Alcohol
  3. Extra
    • Store Bought Ladyfinger

Method:
  • Making the Mascarpone Mix
    1. Add all the Mascarpone (room temperature) into a bowl
    2. Add all the Condensed Milk and Vanilla extract into said bowl
    3. Lightly fold the three components into the bowl till a smooth mix of one colour forms
  • Making the Heavy Cream Mix
    1. Prepare a chilled bowl; a steel/conductive bowl inside a larger bowl holding ice cubes and cold water
    2. Add the Heavy Cream and Sugar into this chilled bowl. (Note, the heavy cream should be chilled at this point already)
    3. Use a beater to beat this mix until you see stiff peaks as you're removing the beater
    4. Once so, add the Mascarpone Mix into this chilled bowl with this mix and lightly fold
  • Making the Coffee Syrup
    1. Add the 15g Sugar into the Black Coffee
    2. Add your choice of alcohol in next
    3. Beat the new mix as if its eggs to get the sugar well mixed
  • Assembly
    1. Dip the ladyfinger into the Coffee and take them out before they break (it'll be quick)
    2. Line the ladyfinger individually across a box/container, once one row is full, pour a bit of the coffee over it again
    3. Add one layer of the batter across the top of these biscuits
    4. Repeat this process according to how many layers you can do (will depend on your bowl size and if you altered the quantity of the ingredients)
    5. Drizzle the topmost layer with cocoa powder if you have it, it adds a nice bitterness to balance out the sweetness
    6. Store it in the fridge to eat whenever you want

Congratulations, you just made yourself a Tiramisu
